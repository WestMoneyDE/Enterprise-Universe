## RECITALS

The Disclosing Party intends to provide the Receiving Party with confidential information in connection with a potential investment in the Company (the "**Transaction**").

The Receiving Party is interested in receiving such information for the purpose of evaluating and assessing a potential investment in the Company.

To protect the confidential information of the Disclosing Party, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## § 1 CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

### 1.1 Definition

"**Confidential Information**" includes all information disclosed by the Disclosing Party to the Receiving Party in connection with the Transaction, including but not limited to:

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Business Information** | Business plans, strategies, financial data, forecasts, budgets |
| **Technical Information** | Software, algorithms, product specifications, roadmaps |
| **Commercial Information** | Customer lists, suppliers, contracts, pricing, pipeline |
| **Legal Information** | Contracts, IP rights, shareholder documents |
| **Personal Data** | Employee information, salaries, organizational charts |
| **Investor Information** | Cap table, term sheets, valuations |

### 1.2 Identification

Confidential Information may be:
- Marked as "**confidential**" in writing or electronically
- Communicated orally and confirmed in writing within **10 business days**
- Recognizably confidential due to its nature or circumstances of disclosure

### 1.3 Exceptions

The following shall not constitute Confidential Information:

- [ ] **a)** Information that was publicly known at the time of disclosure
- [ ] **b)** Information that becomes public after disclosure through no fault of the Receiving Party
- [ ] **c)** Information already lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure
- [ ] **d)** Information lawfully received from a third party without confidentiality obligation
- [ ] **e)** Information independently developed by the Receiving Party

---

## § 2 CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATION

### 2.1 Basic Obligation

The Receiving Party agrees to:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                             │
│   (a) Keep Confidential Information strictly confidential                  │
│                                                                             │
│   (b) Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of evaluating    │
│       the Transaction                                                       │
│                                                                             │
│   (c) Not disclose Confidential Information to third parties               │
│                                                                             │
│   (d) Apply at least the same degree of care as for its own confidential   │
│       information, but in no event less than reasonable care               │
│                                                                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 2.2 Permitted Disclosure

The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to:

| Recipient | Condition |
|-----------|-----------|
| Employees | Need-to-know basis, bound by written confidentiality |
| Legal Counsel | For legal advice, bound by professional duty |
| Tax Advisors | For tax advice, bound by professional duty |
| Auditors | For audit purposes, bound by professional duty |
| Investment Committee | Need-to-know basis, bound by written confidentiality |

### 2.3 Responsibility

The Receiving Party shall be liable for breaches by its representatives, advisors, and employees as if they were its own actions.

## § 3 USE RESTRICTION

### 3.1 Permitted Purpose

The Receiving Party may use the Confidential Information **exclusively** for:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                             │
│   • Evaluating and assessing a potential investment in the Company         │
│   • Conducting due diligence                                                │
│   • Internal decision-making regarding the Transaction                     │
│                                                                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 3.2 Prohibited Use

The Receiving Party shall **not** use Confidential Information for:

- [ ] Competitive purposes
- [ ] Development of competing products or services
- [ ] Solicitation of employees, customers, or business partners
- [ ] Securities trading (insider trading)
- [ ] Any other purposes not directly related to the Transaction

---

## § 4 RETURN AND DESTRUCTION

### 4.1 Upon Termination

Upon request by the Disclosing Party or termination of discussions regarding the Transaction, the Receiving Party shall promptly:

| Action | Deadline |
|--------|----------|
| Return all Confidential Information | 10 business days |
| Destroy or delete all copies | 10 business days |
| Provide written confirmation of destruction | 15 business days |

### 4.2 Exceptions

Exempt from the return/destruction obligation are:
- Copies in automatic backup systems (subject to continuing confidentiality)
- Copies required to be retained under statutory retention requirements
